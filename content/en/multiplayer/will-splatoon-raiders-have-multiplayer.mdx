The gameplay in Splatoon Raiders feels like a natural evolution of the "Side Order" mechanics, but on a much larger scale. One of the most exciting additions is the inclusion of actual turrets. ## Facing the Mutated Salmonid Threat

The primary antagonists in Splatoon Raiders are the mutated salmonids. These aren't your typical Smallfry or Cohocks. The mutations have granted them new abilities, such as the ability to resist standard ink types or deploy their own counter-measures. This increased threat level is the main reason why the co-op mode is so vital. 

Strategies that worked in Salmon Run might not be effective here. Players will need to coordinate their loadouts, with one player focusing on turret defense while another utilizes the midair jump to scout the environment. The question of **will splatoon raiders have multiplayer** depth is answered by these complex enemy encounters that require more than just mindless shooting.

### Enemy Types and Strategies

1.  **Armored Scrapper:** Requires high-damage ink to break the outer shell before the core is exposed.
2.  **Ink-Eater:** A new variant that actively cleans player ink from the floor, limiting mobility.
3.  **Aerial Mutants:** Enemies that stay above the ground, making the midair jump tank upgrade mandatory for survival.

## FAQ

**Q: Will splatoon raiders have multiplayer support for local split-screen?**

A: Currently, the game is confirmed for online and local wireless co-op. There has been no official confirmation regarding a vertical or horizontal split-screen mode for a single console.

**Q: Can I play the entire game solo if I don't want to use co-op?**

A: Yes, Splatoon Raiders is designed as a single-player focused game. While co-op is a major feature, the entire story and all missions are fully playable and balanced for a solo experience.

**Q: Are there competitive modes like Turf War in Splatoon Raiders?**

A: No, Splatoon Raiders is strictly a PvE (Player vs. Environment) experience. For competitive PvP, players are encouraged to continue playing the mainline Splatoon 3 titles.

**Q: Do I need a Nintendo Switch Online subscription for co-op?**

A: Yes, like most titles on the platform, an active Nintendo Switch Online membership is required to play the online multiplayer components of Splatoon Raiders.
