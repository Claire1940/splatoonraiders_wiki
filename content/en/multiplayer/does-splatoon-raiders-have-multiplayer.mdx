## Understanding the "Mechanic" and Customization

In Splatoon Raiders, you aren't just a soldier; you are a specialized operative. The game introduces the concept of the "Mechanic," a role that emphasizes the use of deployable turrets and tank upgrades. When playing in multiplayer, having one player focus on the Mechanic role while others act as frontline Raiders creates a balanced team composition.

The customization options have also been vastly expanded. Octolings finally receive a suite of new hairstyles, and Inklings have access to midair jump abilities that can be equipped on any ink tank. This level of depth allows teams to create unique "meta" strategies for the game's toughest raids.

> **💡 Tip:** In multiplayer sessions, ensure at least one player is running a "Turret Build." Unlike the Sprinklers of old, these actual turrets provide consistent suppressive fire against mutated Salmonids.

## Tank Upgrades and Ability Slots

One of the most exciting additions in the 2026 update to the Splatoon formula is the modular tank system. In previous games, your ink tank was a static piece of equipment. In Splatoon Raiders, the tank is the heart of your build. Each tank has slots for "Chips" that grant passive and active abilities.

### Recommended Multiplayer Chips

1.  **Midair Jump Chip**: Essential for dodging the ground-based shockwaves of mutated bosses.
2.  **Shared Ink Chip**: Allows you to replenish a nearby teammate's ink tank at the cost of a slower personal recharge.
3.  **Turret Efficiency**: Increases the duration and health of deployable Mechanic turrets.
4.  **Damage Number Overlay**: While seemingly cosmetic, this allows the team to see which parts of a boss are taking the most damage in real-time.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when equipping "High-Pressure" chips in multiplayer. While they increase damage, they can inadvertently knock back your teammates, potentially pushing them into enemy ink or off ledges.

## How to Start a Multiplayer Session

Starting a co-op session is straightforward but differs slightly from the lobby system in Splatoon 3. Follow these steps to get your squad together:

1.  Navigate to the **Raider Hub** in the main city.
2.  Interact with the **Deep Cut Terminal**.
3.  Select "Expedition" and choose your desired difficulty.
4.  Choose "Open Room" to allow friends or random players to join, or "Private Room" for a password-protected session.
5.  Confirm your tank loadout and ensure your **Midair Jump** is equipped.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Splatoon Raiders have multiplayer PvP modes like Turf War?**

A: While Splatoon Raiders is primarily focused on cooperative play and the single-player "Raider" campaign, a simplified version of Turf War is available in the social hub. However, the core progression of the game is tied to the co-op missions.

**Q: Can I play Splatoon Raiders entirely solo?**

A: Yes, the game is fully playable solo. The difficulty scales based on the number of players in the session. If you play alone, you may receive AI-controlled "drones" to assist with Mechanic-based tasks.

**Q: Is there local split-screen multiplayer?**

A: As of the 2026 launch, Splatoon Raiders does not support local split-screen. Multiplayer is handled via local wireless (with multiple consoles) or online play.

**Q: How does the "Mechanic" role work in a team?**

A: The Mechanic is a class-like specialization. One player usually takes this role to manage turrets and repair environmental hazards, while the other players focus on "Raiding" (dealing damage and collecting resources). This synergy is the core of the multiplayer experience.
