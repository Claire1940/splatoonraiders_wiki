## The Return of Urchin Underpass

For many veteran players, the most exciting part of the summer 2026 update is the return of Urchin Underpass. Originally the flagship map of the first *Splatoon* game on the Wii U, this map has been redesigned to fit the movement mechanics of *Splatoon 3*. 

While the layout remains familiar to those who played the original, several paths have been widened to accommodate the Squid Roll and Squid Surge. This classic turf provides a nostalgic backdrop for newer players to experience one of the most balanced layouts in series history. Its return to the rotation is expected to significantly impact the Splat Zones and Tower Control modes.

## Competitive Evolution: Series Weapon Power

One of the most technical additions coming in June 2026 is a new stat called "Series Weapon Power." This is specifically designed for the Anarchy Battle series and functions as a sophisticated tracking system for player effectiveness.

This stat tracks your win/loss ratio on a per-weapon basis. Unlike the general rank, Series Weapon Power is a deep dive into how well you perform with specific tools. Most importantly, this stat will now be factored into matchmaking. This change aims to create more balanced matches by ensuring you are pitted against players with similar proficiency levels, preventing "stomps" where one player is significantly more skilled with their chosen weapon than the rest of the lobby.

## Gameplay Innovations in Splatoon Raiders

Moving back to the spin-off, the role of the "Mechanic" is central to the gameplay loop. Unlike the combat-heavy mainline games, *Splatoon Raiders* focuses on using ink as a utility. You might use ink to lubricate rusted gears, conduct electricity through specialized "Ink-Wires," or weight down platforms to solve environmental puzzles.

The Spiralite Islands are filled with "Splat-Tech" remnants. Part of your journey involves scavenging parts to upgrade your equipment. This light RPG element is a first for the franchise and provides a compelling reason to explore every corner of the new map. According to early **splatoon raiders ign** previews, the game will feature a hub world where you can interact with Deep Cut to receive missions and upgrade your gear.
