## Gadget Crafting and Salmoned Parts

A significant departure from previous titles is the introduction of a robust crafting system. Players are no longer limited to what they can buy at Ammo Knights; instead, they must venture out and collect specific "salmoned parts" to build their arsenal. This adds a survival-lite layer to the gameplay, encouraging players to farm specific enemies for components.

For instance, the trailer highlighted the use of window panes salvaged from Scrappers. These parts are essential for crafting high-tech gadgets that can be equipped during missions.

### Common Crafting Components

*   **Scrapper Window Panes:** Used for defensive gadgets and transparent shielding.
*   **Steelhead Saliva:** Likely used for explosive or volatile chemical gadgets.
*   **Smallfry Circuits:** Essential for automated drones or tracking devices.
*   **Drizzler Nozzles:** Used for modifying ink spread and trajectory.

The gadget system allows players to have up to two active gadgets selected at any time. This dual-slot system is reminiscent of classic adventure titles like *The Legend of Zelda: Link's Awakening*, where players must map specific tools to different buttons to solve puzzles or gain tactical advantages in combat.

## The Ship Hideout and Customization

Your central hub in *Splatoon Raiders* is a customizable ship hideout. This vessel serves as your base of operations, where you can craft gear, interact with NPCs, and plan your next raid. The trailer confirmed that the size of this ship hideout can be increased over time, allowing for more rooms and functionality.

One of the most exciting upgrades for the hideout is the addition of a training room. This area allows players to test out their newly crafted gadgets and weapons in a controlled environment before heading out into the field.

### Hideout Upgrade Path

1.  **Level 1: Dinghy Base** – Basic crafting bench and storage.
2.  **Level 2: Raider Sloop** – Unlocks the training room and basic idol quarters.
3.  **Level 3: Ink-Galleon** – Adds the arcade machine and expanded gadget slots.
4.  **Level 4: Dreadnought** – Full customization of the exterior and advanced idol interactions.

The arcade machine mentioned in the trailer is a point of great interest. It may serve as a way to play classic Splatoon mini-games like *Squid Jump*, or it could be a specialized interface for controlling the ship's crane to salvage rare materials from the ocean floor.
