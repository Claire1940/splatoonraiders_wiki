## Weapon Part Swapping and Modifications

One of the most significant shifts in the 2026 gameplay loop is the ability to modify your primary weapons. In previous games, weapons came with fixed sub-weapons and specials. In Splatoon Raiders, the customization extends to the internal components of your ink-sprayers and rollers.

### Modular Weapon Components

By visiting the "Ammo Knights" workshop, players can swap out three main components of their weapon. This adds a layer of tactical **splatoon raiders customization** that allows for personalized playstyles.

1.  **The Nozzle:** Affects ink spread, range, and fire rate.
2.  **The Reservoir:** Determines how much ink is consumed per shot and the reload speed.
3.  **The Trigger Assembly:** Changes the firing mode (e.g., burst fire vs. full auto for certain shooters).

| Component Type | Common Mod | Effect on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nozzle** | Tight-Bore | Increases **Range** while decreasing **Spread** |
| **Reservoir** | High-Capacity | Increases **Ink Tank** efficiency |
| **Trigger** | Rapid-Fire | Increases **DPS** but raises **Recoil** |

## Advanced Ability Management

The 2026 ability system has been overhauled to provide more agency to the player. While RNG (random number generation) still plays a role in gear rolls, the introduction of "Ability Fragments" and "Murch’s Specialized Tools" makes the process of perfecting your gear much smoother.

### Sub-Ability Synergy

When customizing your gear, you should aim for "Synergy Bonuses." Wearing multiple pieces of gear from the same brand or with matching primary abilities can trigger hidden stat boosts. For example, stacking "Ink Recovery Up" across three pieces of gear now provides a 5% bonus to your special gauge charge rate.

| Ability Name | Primary Effect | Strategy Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ink Saver (Main)** | Reduces ink consumption | Essential for **Chargers** and **Splatlings** |
| **Swim Speed Up** | Increases movement in ink | Mandatory for **Frontliners** and **Slayers** |
| **Quick Super Jump** | Faster travel to allies | Best for **Support** players using Beacons |
| **Sub Resistance Up** | Reduces damage from bombs | Highly recommended for **Tower Control** |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when "Scrubbing" gear. While it provides fragments, it resets the gear's experience level, which may temporarily lower your performance in Ranked Battles.

## FAQ

**Q: Does splatoon raiders customization affect my matchmaking?**

A: No, customization is purely based on player preference and gear stats. Your matchmaking is determined by your hidden MMR (Matchmaking Rating) and your current rank in competitive modes, regardless of how your Inkling looks.

**Q: Can I transfer my customization items from Splatoon 3 to Splatoon Raiders?**

A: While most gear does not transfer directly, players with a Splatoon 3 save file on their Nintendo account will unlock a "Legacy Pack" containing classic hairstyles and a set of "Vintage" gear items to use in the 2026 title.

**Q: Are there microtransactions for customization in Splatoon Raiders?**

A: Nintendo has maintained its stance on avoiding traditional microtransactions for gear. Most items are earned through in-game currency (G), Catalog progression, or by participating in Splatfests and Salmon Run shifts.

**Q: How do I unlock the new Backpack slot for customization?**

A: The Backpack slot becomes available once you reach Level 10. You can purchase your first modular ink tank from the "Boiling Point" accessory shop in the main hub.
