## Facing the Mutated Salmonid Threat

The enemies in Splatoon Raiders are a significant departure from the standard Salmonids seen in previous games. These "Mutated Salmonids" are larger, more aggressive, and possess unique abilities that require coordinated team efforts to counter. When engaging in **スプラ トゥーン レイダース マルチプレイ** sessions, players will notice that damage numbers appear over enemies—a feature reminiscent of the Side Order expansion—allowing for more precise DPS (Damage Per Second) calculations.

The expansion of the Salmonid lore also introduces massive bosses that act as progression gates. These encounters often involve multi-stage battles where one player might need to distract the boss while others repair turrets or gather power eggs to fuel a super-weapon.

### Mutated Salmonid Variants

*   **Ink-Eater Scrapper:** A fast-moving variant that actively drains ink trails left by players.
*   **Armored Flyfish:** Requires synchronized hits from two players to disable its thrusters.
*   **Giga-Big Man (Boss):** A mutated version of the fan-favorite character that uses vehicle-based attacks.

## Advanced Tactics for Multiplayer

To excel in **スプラ トゥーン レイダース マルチプレイ**, teams must master the art of "Ink-Linking." This involves creating a continuous path of ink that all players can use to move rapidly across the map. With the addition of the midair jump, verticality is more important than ever. Players should coordinate their jumps to reach high-ground positions where turrets can provide the best coverage.

Another advanced tactic involves the "Turret Overload." If multiple players deploy their turrets in a overlapping radius, the turrets gain a fire-rate bonus. This is particularly effective against the mutated Salmonid hordes that swarm players during the extraction phases of a mission.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Splatoon Raiders support local split-screen for the スプラ トゥーン レイダース マルチプレイ mode?**

A: No, the multiplayer mode is currently restricted to online play and local wireless between multiple consoles to maintain the fluid 60FPS animations and high enemy density.

**Q: Can I play the campaign entirely solo, or is multiplayer required?**

A: While the game is "single-player focused," it is fully playable solo. However, the difficulty scales based on the number of players, and certain encounters are designed to be more manageable with a cooperative team using the **スプラ トゥーン レイダース マルチプレイ** features.

**Q: Are there any new playable races in Splatoon Raiders?**

A: At launch in 2026, players can choose between Inklings and Octolings. The game has introduced a significant number of new hairstyles for both, particularly focusing on the Octoling options which were expanded based on player feedback.

**Q: How do the damage numbers work in co-op?**

A: Similar to the Side Order DLC, damage numbers pop up when you hit enemies. This helps teams identify which player is dealing the most damage and which enemies are "tanky" and require focused fire from the entire group.
