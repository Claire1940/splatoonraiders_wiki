## New Movement Mechanics: Double Jumps and Beyond

The combat in *Splatoon Raiders* is significantly more vertical than its predecessors. The introduction of a dedicated **double jump** and a "mirror jump" indicates that the level design is built around platforming and dodging complex enemy patterns. These movement buffs are essential because the Salmonids have evolved.

Players will encounter new enemy types, such as "Fish Vultures," and returning threats that have been modified for this new environment. The ink itself even looks different—described by some as a "sludge" or "poop ink"—suggesting a more hostile and grimy world than the neon-soaked streets of Splatsville.

## Is PvP Entirely Off the Table?

While the marketing has focused 100% on the adventure aspect, the question of **will splatoon raiders have pvp** remains a topic of speculation. In the gaming industry, developers often lead with the "new" content (in this case, the open-world adventure) before announcing secondary modes.
