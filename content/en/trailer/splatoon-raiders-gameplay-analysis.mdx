## Combat Mechanics: The Splatchet and Gadgets

The "Splatchet" (a hybrid ink-hatchet) is the standout addition to the player's arsenal. This weapon allows for close-quarters combat while maintaining the series' signature ink-slinging mechanics. Players can tinker with, redesign, or unequip the Splatchet at their hideout.

### The 16-Slot Ability System
Weapon customization is governed by a slot system. Each ability has a "cost" associated with it, and players must balance their loadouts within a 16-slot limit. This prevents any single weapon from becoming "overpowered" without significant trade-offs.

| Ability Name | Slot Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Vertical Splash** | 4 Slots | Primary splashes are vertical; increased damage |
| **Overpowered Attacker**| 2 Slots | Increases raw damage output |
| **Emergency Damage Up** | 2 Slots | Boosts damage when player health is low |
| **Explosive Steps** | 3 Slots | Leaves small ink explosions while moving |

In addition to the Splatchet, players can deploy gadgets such as automated turrets launched from portable canisters. These gadgets appear to replace the standard "Sub Weapon" slot found in previous games, offering more utility in the dungeon-crawling format.

## Partner Abilities: Deep Cut Returns

Shiver, Frye, and Big Man aren't just NPCs in Splatoon Raiders; they are active participants in your missions. Each member of Deep Cut provides a unique traversal or combat ability that can be activated during gameplay.

*   **Big Man:** Acts as a mount, allowing players to ride on his back to cross hazardous terrain or move quickly through large zones.
*   **Shiver:** Summons her shark (returning from Splatoon 3) to clear paths or attack clusters of enemies.
*   **Frye:** Her eels return to provide multi-target distractions and crowd control.

The "Hideout Ship" serves as the central hub where these partnerships are managed. By spending collected resources, players can engage in "Hideout Ship Construction" to unlock new rooms, gadget development labs, and relic powers.

## Resource Management and Progression

Progression in Splatoon Raiders is tied to the collection of crystals and shards found within dungeons. Using a specialized drill, players can crack open large blue and amber crystals to reveal chests containing gear and rare abilities, such as the "Midair Jump" (Double Jump).

### Crafting Requirements
To develop advanced tools, players must hoard specific materials. For example, building a high-tier Splatchet variant may require:
1.  **500 Spiral Height Shards**
2.  **5 Scrapper's Glass Windows**

The UI also tracks "Tank Capacity," which seems to be a separate meter from the standard ink tank, possibly limiting how long players can remain in a dungeon or how many special abilities they can trigger before needing to extract or resupply.

> **Warning:** Entering a high-level dungeon (e.g., Level 10) while your character is only Level 4 will result in significantly reduced damage output and a high risk of mission failure.
