## Materials and Textures in Splatoon Raiders

What sets **splatoon raiders hair styles** apart from previous titles is the use of non-ink materials. In the 2026 release, Nintendo has experimented with shaders that make hair look like more than just organic matter.

1. **Metallic Chains:** Used in several braided styles, these provide a heavy, clinking sound effect during movement.
2. **Industrial Plastics:** Items like the plastic fork or zip-ties are used to hold tentacles in place.
3. **Synthetic Cables:** These textures feature a matte, rubberized finish that contrasts with the glossy sheen of the ink.
4. **Organic Seaweed:** A softer alternative to chains, providing a more "naturalist" raider look.

These material changes aren't just for show; they reflect the lore of the Raiders world, where resources are scarce and Inklings must use whatever they find to maintain their fresh look. You can see more about the game's development and world-building on the [official Nintendo website](https://www.nintendo.com), which often details the inspiration behind these design shifts.

## Customizing Your Look

To change your hair style in Splatoon Raiders, you can access the customization menu at any time from the main lobby. Unlike gear, which provides specific ability chunks, hair styles are purely cosmetic. However, they are a vital part of your "Freshness" rating in the community.

Follow these steps to update your look:
*   Open the **Menu** by pressing the X button.
*   Navigate to the **Options** tab.
*   Select **Other** and then **Player Settings**.
*   Choose your species (Inkling or Octoling) and browse the available styles.

> **Warning:** Changing your species will reset your current hair selection, so be sure to re-select your preferred style after switching between Inkling and Octoling.

## The Aesthetic Impact of Raider Styles

The shift toward "Raider" aesthetics represents a significant departure for the franchise. In earlier games, the focus was on high-fashion and street culture. In 2026, the focus has shifted to survival and DIY engineering. This is reflected in the hair styles through the inclusion of "junk" items. The composite cable hair, for instance, suggests that Octolings are integrating salvaged human technology directly into their fashion.

The "Enemy Octoling" vibe mentioned by many players is a deliberate callback to the Octo Expansion, but with a more weathered, battle-worn appearance. This allows players to roleplay as grizzled veterans of the Splatlands, moving away from the "pop star" image of the past.
