Fashion in the Splatoon universe has always been a reflection of the current "vibe" of Inkopolis or Splatsville. ## How to Unlock and Change Hairstyles

In Splatoon Raiders, all twelve base hairstyles are available from the very beginning of the game. During the initial character creation screen, you will be prompted to select your species and your starting hair. However, if you find yourself wanting a change after a few hours of gameplay, the process is simple.

1.  Enter the **Main Menu** while in the central hub.
2.  Navigate to the **Options** tab.
3.  Select **Other**, then choose **Style Settings**.
4.  From here, you can swap between the six styles available for your species at any time.

> **Warning:** Changing your species (Inkling to Octoling) requires a full character reset in the settings menu, though you will retain all your gear, levels, and currency.

## Matching Hairstyles with Gear Abilities

While hairstyles are purely cosmetic, they often dictate the "silhouette" of your character, which can affect how easily you are spotted in certain maps. A high-profile hairstyle like the "Elite Guard Vibe" might make you a slightly more visible target behind low cover compared to the "Composite Crop."

When building your loadout, consider the following:
*   **Stealth Builds:** Use shorter, tighter hairstyles like the "Composite Crop" or "Tangled Twists" to minimize your profile.
*   **Aggressive Builds:** Larger, more intimidating styles like the "Chain-Link Braids" or "Pompadour" can make your character appear more imposing during close-quarters combat.
*   **Support Builds:** Styles with integrated cloth, like the "Bandana Tucked," often blend well with the tactical vests and backpacks used by support players.

**Q: Can I use Inkling hairstyles on an Octoling character?**

A: No, hairstyles are currently species-specific. You must be an Inkling to use the six Inkling styles and an Octoling to use the six Octoling styles. However, you can switch species at any time in the settings menu.

**Q: Does the splatoon raiders hairstyle guide include DLC styles?**

A: This guide covers the twelve base styles available at launch. If Nintendo releases a "Side Order" style expansion or a paid DLC in late 2026, we will update the guide to include those exclusive options.

**Q: Do hairstyles affect my hitboxes in multiplayer?**

A: No, hairstyles are purely cosmetic. Regardless of how large or small your hair appears, your character's hitbox remains consistent with all other players to ensure competitive fairness.
