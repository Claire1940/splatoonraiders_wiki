## Weapon Personalization and Visual Loadouts

For the first time in the series, weapons are part of the **splatoon raiders customization options** suite beyond just their functional stats. While you cannot change the base fire rate or damage of a Splattershot, you can now apply "Weapon Wraps" and "Charms."

- **Weapon Wraps:** These are skins that change the color and texture of your weapon. Popular 2026 styles include the "Sun-Bleached Neon" and "Rusty Scavenger" looks.
- **Charms:** Small 3D trinkets that dangle from the handle of your weapon. These are often rewarded for reaching high freshness ratings with a specific weapon class.
- **Ink Color Presets:** While ink color is usually determined by the match, players can set a "Lobby Color" that shows up in the social hub and during weapon testing.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Some weapon wraps may be harder to see in certain lighting conditions. Always test your visibility in the firing range before heading into a Ranked Battle.

## The Social Space: Lockers and Splashtags

Customization isn't just for your character's body; it extends to how you are perceived in the community. The Locker Room returns in Splatoon Raiders with double the vertical space of previous titles, allowing for more complex interior decorating.

## How to Unlock New Cosmetic Items

To fully explore all **splatoon raiders customization options**, you will need to engage with several different progression systems. The most direct method is visiting the shops in the main hub, but the most exclusive items are found elsewhere.

- **The Catalog:** A seasonal battle pass that is free for all players. By playing matches and earning experience, you unlock tiers containing exclusive gear, furniture, and emotes.
- **Raider Runs:** A new PvE mode where players scavenge for "Sunken Treasure." These chests often contain unique "Raider" themed gear that cannot be bought in shops.
- **Amiibo Support:** Scanning compatible Amiibo figures unlocks legacy outfits from Splatoon 1, 2, and 3, updated with 2026-standard textures and physics.

For more information on official updates and seasonal events, visit the [official Nintendo Splatoon portal](https://www.nintendo.com) to stay up to date with the latest patches.

## Advanced Style: The Freshness System

In Splatoon Raiders, "Freshness" is a tangible stat that increases as you use specific gear combinations. Higher Freshness levels unlock "Style Perks," which are purely cosmetic effects like a shimmering aura around your character or unique ink-trail patterns.

To maximize your Freshness, try to wear gear from the same brand. Brands like *Tentatek* and *Forge* return with new 2026 summer collections that provide a "Brand Synergy" bonus, making your character glow slightly during the match intro.

> **💡 Tip:** Use the "Photo Mode" in the hub world to capture your latest outfit. You can apply filters and poses to create the perfect profile picture for your Splashtag.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I transfer my customization items from Splatoon 3 to Splatoon Raiders?**

A: While you cannot transfer every item, Nintendo has confirmed that players with Splatoon 3 save data on their Nintendo Account will unlock a "Veteran's Trunk" containing classic gear and a special "Splatlands Survivor" title.

**Q: Do the splatoon raiders customization options affect my character's hitboxes?**

A: No. Regardless of whether you choose a bulky "Raider" jacket or a slim swimsuit, the character hitboxes remain consistent to ensure competitive fairness.

**Q: How often do the clothing shops rotate their inventory?**

A: The shops in the main hub rotate every 24 hours at 00:00 UTC. However, you can also order gear from other players you see in the plaza by talking to Murch.

**Q: Are there any gender-locked customization options in Splatoon Raiders?**

A: No. All hairstyles, clothing, and voice options are available to all players regardless of their initial character selection, following the series' tradition of inclusive style.
