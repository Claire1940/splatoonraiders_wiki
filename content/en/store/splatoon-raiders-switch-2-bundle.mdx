## The Evolution of Switch 2 Physical Media

A major concern for the Switch 2 generation has been the prevalence of "Key Cards." These are physical boxes sold at retail that contain a cartridge acting only as a license key, requiring a massive digital download to actually play the game. This has been a point of contention for collectors who value game preservation. However, recent teardowns of new cartridges, such as the one for the Metroidvania title *Mio*, show a new "G4" marking.

The transition from G6 (64GB) to G4 (likely 16GB) cartridges suggests that Nintendo is diversifying its storage options. By offering smaller, cheaper cartridges, publishers of indie and mid-tier games can afford to put the full game on the physical media rather than relying on the "Key Card" model. This is excellent news for the **splatoon raiders switch 2 bundle**, as it increases the likelihood of the game being fully playable right out of the box without a 50GB day-one patch.
