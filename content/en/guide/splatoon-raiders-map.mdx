## Exploring the Splatoon Raiders Map: Spiralite Islands

The **splatoon raiders map** is fundamentally different from the symmetrical arenas found in the main series. Set within the Spiralite Islands, this map emphasizes exploration, verticality, and environmental interaction. As a mechanic, your role involves more than just splatting enemies; you must interact with the terrain to progress. The islands are rumored to feature a variety of biomes, ranging from sun-bleached beaches to dense, ink-slicked jungles.

Unlike the urban sprawl of Inkopolis or Splatsville, the Spiralite Islands offer a more organic, rugged terrain. Players will need to use their mechanical tools to bridge gaps, power up ancient machinery, and create shortcuts. This shift toward an adventure-style map means that memorizing the "flow" of the islands is just as important as having a quick trigger finger.

## The Return of Urchin Underpass in Splatoon 3

While Splatoon Raiders looks toward the future, the Splatoon 3 update scheduled for June 12, 2026, pays homage to the series' roots. The iconic **Urchin Underpass** map is making its triumphant return. Originally featured in the first Splatoon game, this map is beloved for its balanced layout and intense central chokepoint.

The 2026 version of Urchin Underpass has been meticulously updated to fit the Splatoon 3 movement suite. This means players can now utilize Squid Rolls and Squid Surges to navigate the side alleys and central ramparts. The visual fidelity has also been cranked up, especially for those playing on the Nintendo Switch 2, where the concrete textures and ink reflections appear more lifelike than ever.

### Key Features of the Updated Urchin Underpass
1.  **Redesigned Central Zone:** The middle of the map now features more cover options to prevent long-range chargers from dominating the match.
2.  **Enhanced Verticality:** New grates and climbable surfaces have been added to the side routes, allowing for more creative flanking maneuvers.
3.  **Dynamic Lighting:** Depending on the time of day in-game, the shadows under the bridge can drastically change visibility, adding a new layer of strategy.

## The Splatlands Collection: New Weapons and Brands

The June 12 update doesn't just bring back old maps; it also introduces the **Splatlands Collection**. This collection features 30 weapons in total, primarily from the Barazushi and Embers brands. These aren't just simple re-skins; they feature entirely different sub-weapons and specials, fundamentally changing how you approach any given **splatoon raiders map** or Splatoon 3 stage.

### Barazushi and Embers Highlights
The Barazushi brand continues its trend of rugged, survivalist-themed gear, while Embers focuses on high-heat, high-intensity weaponry. These brands are perfect for the harsh environments of the Spiralite Islands.

*   **Barazushi Splatana:** Features a wider horizontal swing but a slower vertical charge.
*   **Embers Dualies:** Leaves a lingering heat trail that deals minor damage to enemies who step in it.
*   **Barazushi Charger:** Includes a built-in scope that highlights enemies through thin walls of ink.

> **Warning:** With the introduction of the "Series Weapon Power" stat, matchmaking will be tighter than ever. Ensure you practice with these new weapons in Turf War before taking them into Anarchy Battles.

## New Ranking Systems and Badges

To keep the competitive scene fresh, the Squid Research Lab is raising the freshness caps on all weapons. This allows veteran players to continue showing off their dedication to their favorite gear. Additionally, the new **Series Weapon Power** stat is being integrated into Anarchy Battle series.

This stat tracks your effectiveness with a specific weapon based on your win-loss ratio. The matchmaking system will then use this data to pair you with and against players of similar skill levels. This should lead to much closer games, though it may increase queue times for those at the very top of the skill ceiling.

### Earnable Badges in 2026
*   **Spiralite Explorer:** Awarded for uncovering 100% of the **splatoon raiders map**.
*   **Barazushi Master:** Reach the new freshness cap with any five Barazushi weapons.
*   **Underpass Veteran:** Win 50 matches on the returning Urchin Underpass stage.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Splatoon Raiders map open world?**

A: While not a traditional open world like Zelda, the **splatoon raiders map** consists of large, interconnected "Spiralite Islands" that offer much more freedom of movement and exploration than the standard Splatoon stages.

**Q: Can I play Splatoon Raiders on the original Nintendo Switch?**

A: No, Splatoon Raiders is an exclusive title for the Nintendo Switch 2 system to take advantage of the increased processing power required for the larger maps and detailed visuals. However, the Splatoon 3 update is available on both consoles.

**Q: When does Urchin Underpass return to Splatoon 3?**

A: Urchin Underpass is scheduled to return as part of the free Splatlands Collection update on June 12, 2026.

**Q: What is the "Series Weapon Power" stat?**

A: This is a new performance metric that tracks your win/loss ratio with specific weapons. It is used to improve matchmaking accuracy in Anarchy Battles, ensuring you face opponents with similar weapon proficiency.
