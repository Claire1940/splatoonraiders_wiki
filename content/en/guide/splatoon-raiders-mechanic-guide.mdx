## The Home Base: Upgrading the Raft

The heart of your journey is your mobile home base—a raft constructed from the remnants of your downed helicopter. Because inklings cannot survive in deep water, the raft acts as your only safe haven and primary mode of transportation between the different biomes of the Spiralite Islands.

One of the most critical components of your raft is the **Rotor Crane**. Repurposed from the helicopter’s main rotor, this crane allows you to fish for submerged treasures and resources that would otherwise be unreachable. As you progress, you will need to upgrade the raft to withstand harsher environments, such as the freezing temperatures of the glacier islands or the jagged rocks of the stony biomes.

### Raft Upgrade Tiers

| Tier | Required Materials | New Capabilities |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: Makeshift Plank** | Helicopter Scrap (x10) | Basic flotation, manual paddling. |
| **Tier 2: Rotor-Powered** | Rotor Blades, Copper Wire | Unlocks the Crane; faster movement. |
| **Tier 3: Armored Hull** | Reinforced Steel, Ink Sealant | Can traverse stony reefs without damage. |
| **Tier 4: Thermal Engine** | Glacier Cores, High-Grade Oil | Allows entry into the Snowy Glacier Island. |

## Crafting Makeshift Weaponry

In Splatoon Raiders, you don't just buy a Splattershot from Sheldon; you build it. The mechanic uses "thingabobs" and household waste to create functional ink weaponry. For example, your starting weapon is a makeshift Splattershot constructed from a discarded water bottle and various hoses.

Your sub-weapons are also crafted from salvaged parts. A common early-game sub-weapon is the **Makeshift Sprinkler**, which appears to be a repurposed desk fan with three hoses attached. These tools are essential for fending off the local wildlife and any potential Salmonid threats that may have migrated to the islands.

### Essential Crafting Components
*   **Water Bottles:** Used as the primary pressurized chamber for ink weapons.
*   **Hoses & Tubing:** Critical for connecting ink tanks to nozzles.
*   **Pots and Lids:** Often used to reinforce your back-mounted ink tank.
*   **Fans:** The base component for area-of-effect sub-weapons like sprinklers.

## Exploring the Spiralite Islands

The game world is structured as a semi-open world, similar to the "Side Order" or Splatoon 3's "Alterna," but on a much larger scale. The islands are arranged around a central objective, with each island offering unique resources required for your next set of upgrades.

| Biome | Key Resources | Environment Hazard |
|---|---|---|
| **Grassy/Jungle Islands** | Wood, Vines, Basic Scrap | Dense foliage blocking visibility. |
| **Stony Islands** | Minerals, Hardened Steel | Sharp rocks that can damage the raft. |
| **Snowy Glacier Island** | Ice Crystals, Thermal Energy | Freezing water that drains ink levels. |

As you explore, you will be accompanied by the members of Deep Cut. Each member seems to provide a specific utility to the mechanic. Shiver often handles the fishing and maritime navigation, while Big Man appears to assist with deep-sea operations using a specialized "Submarine Buddy." This submarine allows the mechanic to view underwater environments through a monitor, bypassing the inkling's natural weakness to water.

## Survival Strategy and Resource Management

To succeed in your role, you must balance exploration with maintenance. Resource gathering is not just about finding the next upgrade; it is about keeping your current gear functional. Every tool has a durability rating, and your makeshift weapons will require periodic repairs using the scrap you find in the field.

1.  **Prioritize the Crane:** The Rotor Crane is your most consistent source of high-quality scrap. Spend your first few hours on the grassy islands focusing on crane upgrades.
2.  **Scout with the Submarine:** Before landing on a new island, use the Submarine Buddy to check the surrounding waters for hazards or sunken crates.
3.  **Manage Your Tank:** Since you are using a makeshift ink tank made of pots and lids, your ink recovery is slower than standard military-grade gear. Use your ink sparingly during PvE encounters.

> **Warning:** Venturing too far from your raft without a "Return Beacon" can be fatal. If you fall into the water without your raft nearby, you will lose a portion of your collected resources.

## The Role of Deep Cut

Deep Cut isn't just there for moral support; they are active participants in your survival story. Each member has adapted to the survivalist lifestyle on the Spiralite Islands.

*   **Shiver:** Acts as the primary scout and fisher. Her expertise with a fishing rod helps in gathering organic resources and food.
*   **Frye:** Specializes in rapid resource collection and agility, often helping the mechanic reach high-altitude scrap piles.
*   **Big Man:** The "Deep Sea Navigator." He is often seen in survival gear, managing the submarine and helping the mechanic navigate the deeper, more dangerous channels between islands.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Splatoon Raiders a multiplayer game like the main series?**

A: While the main Splatoon series focuses on 4v4 multiplayer, Splatoon Raiders is primarily a single-player survival-crafting adventure. There may be cooperative elements introduced later, but the core experience is built around the solo mechanic's journey.

**Q: Do I need a Switch 2 to play Splatoon Raiders?**

A: Yes, Splatoon Raiders has been announced as a Switch 2 exclusive. It utilizes the increased processing power of the new hardware to render the semi-open world of the Spiralite Islands and the complex physics of the raft mechanics.

**Q: How does the crafting in this splatoon raiders mechanic guide differ from weapon shops?**

A: In this game, there are no shops. You must find blueprints and raw materials (scrap, bottles, fans) in the environment. You then use a crafting bench on your raft to assemble and upgrade your gear manually.

**Q: What happened to the Inklings' ability to transform into squids?**

A: The mechanic can still transform into a squid to move through ink, but since the environment is mostly water and sand, you must create "ink paths" using your makeshift tools to move effectively. Transforming in water is still fatal, making the raft your primary platform for movement.
