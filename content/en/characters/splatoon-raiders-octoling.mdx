## Open-Zoned Exploration and the Floating Ship

Splatoon Raiders departs from the traditional hub-and-spoke level design. Instead, the game utilizes an "open-zoned" structure. You navigate a sprawling map of floating islands using a customizable floating ship. This vessel acts as your mobile base of operations, where you plan your next move and upgrade your equipment.

The ship itself is not just a menu; it is a gameplay mechanic. As you progress through 2026, you will collect materials to upgrade the ship’s speed, storage capacity, and defensive turrets. These upgrades allow you to reach more distant islands that house rarer treasure and more difficult Salmonid bosses.

### High-Stakes Raiding Mechanics
The core of the game revolves around "Raids." These are high-stakes missions where the primary goal is to extract treasure. Unlike previous modes, if you fail a raid, the rewards are significantly diminished, making every encounter feel meaningful.

1.  **Select a Zone:** Use the map on your ship to pick a destination.
2.  **Deploy with Support:** Choose a member of Deep Cut to provide mechanical assistance.
3.  **Infiltrate Dungeons:** Enter localized instances filled with puzzles and enemies.
4.  **Extract Treasure:** Reach the extraction point before your ink reserves or health are depleted.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I play as a splatoon raiders octoling from the very beginning?**

A: Yes, the game allows you to choose between being an Inkling or an Octoling during the initial character creation screen without needing to unlock the species through DLC.

**Q: Is Splatoon Raiders an open-world game?**

A: Not exactly. It is described as "Open Zoned." While you have a large map and a ship to travel between islands, each island acts as a self-contained mission area rather than one continuous landmass.

**Q: How does the "High Stakes" mechanic work?**

A: In high-stakes raids, failing to reach the extraction point results in the loss of most crafting materials and treasure found during that specific run. However, your permanent gear and skill tree progress remain intact.

**Q: Do the new Salmonid types appear in the standard Salmon Run mode?**

A: Splatoon Raiders focuses on the raid adventure mode, but the new flying Salmonid types are integrated into the combat encounters across all major gameplay zones in the 2026 release.
